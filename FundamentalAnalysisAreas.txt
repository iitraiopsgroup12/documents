# Sections for Fundamental Analysis

1. **Financial Results**
   Quarterly and yearly profit/loss, revenue, margins, EPS, cash flow indicators.

2. **Annual Reports**
   Most comprehensive source: management discussion, business model, risks, strategy, segment data.

3. **Annual Reports XBRL**
   Structured financial data useful for automated analysis.

4. **Announcements**
   Real-time corporate updates affecting valuation and business outlook.

5. **Board Meetings**
   Helps track earnings dates, dividends, fund raising, mergers, etc.

6. **Corporate Actions**
   Bonus issues, stock splits, dividends, buybacks, rights issues.

7. **Shareholding Patterns**
   Promoter holding, FII/DII participation, public ownership trends.

8. **Shareholding Patterns SDD**
   More structured disclosures of holdings and changes.

9. **Related Party Transactions**
   Important for governance quality and possible fund diversion risks.

10. **Corporate Governance**
    Board independence, governance standards, compliance quality.

11. **Investor Complaints**
    Signals operational transparency and shareholder servicing quality.

12. **Statement on Impact of Audit Qualification**
    Detects accounting concerns and audit red flags.

13. **Secretarial Compliance**
    Regulatory compliance and governance discipline.

14. **Business Responsibility and Sustainability Report**
    ESG analysis, sustainability practices, long-term risk assessment.

15. **Insider Trading**
    Promoter/management buying or selling activity.

16. **Insider Trading-Plan**
    Planned insider trades that may indicate management expectations.

17. **Shareholder’s Meetings**
    Resolutions, voting patterns, governance decisions.

18. **Voting Results**
    Reveals shareholder support or resistance to company resolutions.

19. **Statement of Deviation/Variation**
    Whether raised funds are being used as promised.

20. **Event Calendar**
    Useful for tracking earnings announcements and important corporate events.

---

# High-Priority Bonus Sections (Situationally Important)

These are extremely valuable for specific types of companies/events:

* **Rights & QIP** → Capital raising analysis
* **Preferential Issue** → Dilution and promoter activity
* **Debt Centralised Database** → Debt repayment and credit quality
* **Credit Rating Details** → Financial stability and leverage risk
* **Default Payment Details** → Serious red flag indicator
* **Regulation 29 / 31 (SAST)** → Promoter stake changes and pledging
* **Pledged Data** → Promoter share pledge risk
* **Scheme of Arrangements** → Mergers, demergers, restructuring
* **Information Memorandum** → Debt issue details and fundraising disclosures
